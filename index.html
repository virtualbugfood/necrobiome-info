WHAT IS THIS

NECROBIOME is a cyberpunk Conway's Game of Life simulator where every cell is a unique creature with its own genome, diet, energy, memories, and ambitions.

In the original Game of Life every cell is identical -- alive or dead, nothing more. In NECROBIOME every live cell is a creature with a 16-character genome encoding its diet, metabolism, aggression, resilience, and sociability. The Conway birth and death rules still apply, but they are bent and broken by who these creatures are and where they live.

They are born. They eat. They build. They hunt. They grow old. They die. Some of them learn. Some evolve into something you have never seen before.

You did not design them. You cannot fully control them. You can only watch, intervene, and wonder.

THE BIOMES

Five natural biomes are painted across the grid using layered noise. Boundaries are soft and organic. Creatures that survive long enough in one biome develop traits suited to it. A sixth biome -- Personal Cyberspace -- does not exist at world start and must be unlocked through evolution.

THE GENOME

Every creature carries a 16-character genome that encodes everything about it. Something like AX3#KRONB!YQ2@ZM. Each character is a gene. Where a character falls in the gene pool determines its value -- near the start means low, near the end means high.

You can copy a genome by pressing C on any creature, paste it with P, or write a custom genome with B. Children inherit mutated copies of their parent's genome. Most mutations are tiny. Occasionally they are dramatic.

DIET

HERBIVORE

Eat biome food at 1.6× the base rate. Efficient, peaceful, industrious -- the best builders. Terrible hunters. Survive by being numerous. When carnivores arrive, they collapse.

OMNIVORE

The generalists. Base food rate plus moderate predation energy. Build at normal pace. Can survive almost anywhere. Most starting populations are majority omnivore.

CARNIVORE

2× energy from kills. Gain almost nothing from biome food. Hunt the 8 neighboring cells each tick. Get a 1.5× hunt bonus at night. A world of pure carnivores always collapses -- they need prey.

The world runs on a 300-tick day/night cycle. Day creatures gain bonus energy in daylight, night creatures in the dark. Carnivores get a hunt bonus when the world goes dark.

STRUCTURES

Creatures harvest materials each tick they survive in a biome, then spend them on structures. Structures decay and will collapse if no creature nearby repairs them. Social creatures (sociability above 0.65) place blueprints on adjacent empty cells -- family members automatically contribute materials until the build is funded, then it constructs itself.

PERSONAL CYBERSPACE

Deep in the substrate, past the Void and beneath the Data Reef, there exists a place that the other biomes do not know about. Personal Cyberspace is not a natural biome. It was made. Someone -- or something -- partitioned a section of the world and filled it with the residue of their own consciousness.

How It Opens

Cyberspace does not exist at world start. It awakens when evolution goes deep enough -- when any creature reaches generation 40 with 25 guided adaptations. A rectangular pocket tears open somewhere on the map, overwriting whatever biome was there. The grid turns deep violet. The ticker reads:

// SYSTEM BREACH -- Personal Cyberspace opened //

The Shifting Rules

The Conway rules inside Cyberspace rewrite themselves every 60 ticks from a pool of 13 configurations. What kept a creature alive last generation may kill it this one.

Memory Fragments

The food source of Cyberspace. Creatures with fewer than 5 guided adaptations gain almost nothing and starve within ~30 ticks. Creatures with 5–14 sustain themselves. Creatures with 15+ feast -- memory fragments are the richest food source in the world for lineages that earned the right to eat them.

Glitch Events

Every 40 ticks one creature inside Cyberspace is selected at random and something happens to it.

TIMESTOP

Outside time for 8 ticks. Cannot die from any cause.

SPLICE

Three random genes overwritten instantly. Diet may change. Everything changes.

TELEPORT

Displaced to a random empty cell inside Cyberspace.

ECHO

Spawns a child on an adjacent empty cell regardless of Conway rules.

The Architect

The first creature to survive 50 continuous ticks inside Cyberspace earns the title [ARCH]. Its name is prefixed permanently. Its genome is logged. There can only ever be one Architect per session. When you see [ARCH:name] appear in the HUD, find that creature immediately.

GAME MODES

Select a mode from the title screen using the arrow keys. Most modes are locked at first and must be unlocked through play. Unlock progress persists between sessions.

@ CLASSIC always available

The standard experience. Five biomes, standard Conway rules, normal pacing. Everything in this document describes Classic mode.

% VOLATILE unlock: reach tick 2000

Mutation rate tripled. Hazard cooldowns cut to 45% of normal. Acid Floods arrive every hundred ticks. Genomes drift fast. Mass extinctions are common. So are sudden population explosions. Volatile rewards watching, not planning.

- DEEP TIME unlock: generation 50

Slow tick rate. Max age raised to 2000 ticks. A creature with max age 2000 can live for over 16 real-time minutes. Evolution is visible in Deep Time in a way it never is in other modes. You can watch a lineage specialize over hours.

# OVERPOPULATED unlock: build 20 structures

Population target raised to 2000. The grid is dense from tick one and never quiets down. Resources are thin. Competition is constant. Structures matter more here than in any other mode.

. VOID ONLY unlock: crown an Architect

A single biome covers the entire world. No Data Reef, no Acid Delta, no Chrome Flats. Only the Void. What survives here evolved for exactly this -- nothing -- and earned every tick.

DAEMON MODE

Something is still running in the old substrate. Not creatures -- those evolved on their own. Not Cyberspace -- that was built deliberately. Something older. Background threads that never terminated when the original system went dark. You found the command interface. You can issue directives.

Daemon Mode is a completely different game from Classic. You control named Daemons -- persistent substrate processes with genomes, memories, and relationships -- directing them to explore, harvest, mine, craft, and build in a 210x122 procedurally generated tile world across 11 biomes. The world is different every run.

Unlock: crown an Architect and build a Home in the same Classic session.

SYSTEMS

Mining

Press M on a Ruin tile to mine it. 17 ruin types with unique drop tables -- some spawn a RELAY tile on exhaustion, others burst with materials.

Crafting

Press C to open the craft menu. 105 recipes, filtered to what you can currently make. A Synthesis Bench gives 30% faster crafting and unlocks advanced recipes.

Structures

59 buildable structures. Craft the item then press V to place it. A closed ring of Signal Walls with an Access Port creates a Secure Enclave -- Daemons inside passively regen priority.

Relationships

Each Daemon tracks affinity (-100 to +100) with every other. Proximity raises it, competing for fauna lowers it. High-affinity pairs move faster near each other.

Examine Mode

Press E to pause and inspect anything -- tile lore, ruin durability, fauna lifecycle, structure contents, Daemon stats. Essential for knowing what you're dealing with.

Equipment

40 wearable items across five slots: SHELL, HEAD, FEET, CACHE, and TOOL. Notable: Reef Suit removes water penalty, Void Plate eliminates Crystal Wastes drain, Resonance Mantle pulses regen to nearby allies.

THINGS TO WATCH FOR

The first Acid Flood

Around tick 220, the Acid Delta erupts. A wave of deaths followed by a green energy surge across the survivors. Watch their children -- they inherit the strength.

Carnivores at night

Zoom into the Neon Wastes during the dark phase. If there is a healthy carnivore population you will see them hunting. Creatures go orange, then red, then vanish.

The first Home

Around tick 400–500, a social creature with enough materials places the first H. It glows gold. Press I on it. Read how old it is. Read its lineage depth.

A Blueprint being funded

A dim outline glyph on an empty cell, slowly brightening over dozens of ticks. Nearby creatures are contributing materials. When it reaches full brightness, the structure appears instantly.

Biome border wars

The boundary between Acid Delta and Chrome Flats. Resilient aggressive creatures on one side, slow stable ones on the other. The border shifts over hundreds of ticks.

The Cyberspace breach

Watch the ticker. When a lineage reaches generation 40 with 25 adaptations, a violet rectangle tears open somewhere on the map. Move to it. The glyphs inside are Greek letters. The rules change every 60 ticks.

A Splice event

A creature inside Cyberspace suddenly changes glyph. Its genome was rewritten mid-life. Press I immediately. Look at the new traits. It is still the same creature. The world just rewrote what that means.

The Architect being crowned

[ARCH:name] appears in the HUD. That creature survived 50 ticks inside Cyberspace across rule shifts, glitch events, and starvation. Find it. Read its genome. That genome survived something.

Genome drift

Copy a creature's genome at tick 100. Let the simulation run to tick 1000. Find a descendant. Compare the genomes. The drift is the entire story of what happened in between.

NECROBIOME is an open simulation. It does not have a win state.

It has only the next tick. And the tick after that.

And whatever emerges from the space between them.
